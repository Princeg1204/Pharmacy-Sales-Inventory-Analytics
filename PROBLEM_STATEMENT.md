# Project Problem Statement

## 1. Current Business Challenges
Retail pharmacy operations face key execution bottlenecks:
- **Inventory Balancing**: Holding excess inventory ties up working capital. Conversely, stocking too little leads to stockouts of life-critical drugs.
- **Customer Churn Risk**: Standard POS metrics do not identify chronic care patients who have missed regular prescription refills.
- **Seasonal Inefficiencies**: Supply chains are caught off-guard during cold/flu and allergy peaks.
- **Product Portfolio Blindspots**: Managers lack clear insights on which products represent "Cash Cows" versus "Dogs".

## 2. Data-Driven Questions to Answer
Our analytics system must address:
- Which products generate the top 80% of revenue?
- Which categories have the slowest inventory turnover?
- Which SKUs require immediate purchasing alerts?
- How do weekday sales patterns vary?
- Who are the high-value (VIP) customers and which ones are "At-Risk"?

## 3. Success Criteria
- **Pipeline Automation**: End-to-end data ingestion, validation, and feature calculation running under 1 minute.
- **Data Quality assurance**: Automated logs reporting duplicate rows, null imputations, and outlier capped percentages.
- **Actionable Visuals**: High-fidelity dashboard sheets displaying financial KPIs, inventory statuses, and product matrices.

## 4. Expected Outcomes
- **15-20% Reduction** in inventory holding costs through reorder point tuning.
- **10% Improvement** in chronic-patient retention using CLV segment lists.
- **Minimized Stockouts** on critical medications, enhancing store reputation and sales.
